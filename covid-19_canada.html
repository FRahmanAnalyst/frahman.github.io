COVID-19 Canada -
Vaccinations, Deaths and Infections

Cleaning and joining Covid-19 data in Microsoft SQL Server and visualized in Tableau,
to show the timeline and comparison of vaccines, infections and deaths across Canadian
provinces and territories, until the first week of July.

This data analysis project was aimed at comparing and visualizing COVID-19 infections, deaths and vaccinations across Canada up until the first week of July 2021. It compared the infection rates, vaccinations and mortality across Canada’s provinces and territories and also compared the mortality rates against infection rates, infection rates against vaccination rates and the morality rates against vaccination rates. The data used in this project was obtained from the GitHub page of the COVID-19 Canada Open Data Working Group.

COVID-19 Canada Open Data Working Group GitHub Page

The first step involved combining the required columns from the data tables in Microsoft SQL Server using views and join commands. Then the data was checked for duplicates and irrelevant data like ‘repatriated cases’ were removed. The SQL commands used, are shown below as well as the download link for the SQL script.

Download SQL Script

The new created table with the cleaned data was imported into Tableau Public to create the dashboard below. It compares the infection rates (percentage of population infected with COVID-19), mortality rates (percentage of deaths from infections) as well as the vaccination rates (percentage of population who received two doses of the COVID-19 vaccine) across provinces and territories. The dashboard also visualizes the timeline of infections against mortality, the rate of vaccinations per week, infection rate against vaccinations and mortality rate against vaccinations.

View on Tableau Public

The visualized data shows that, as of the first week of July 2021, the Province of Quebec had the highest mortality rate in Canada, followed by Manitoba and New Brunswick, while Alberta had the highest infection rate. The territories led in the percentage of fully vaccinated populace, with Yukon coming in at first with 71.16% of its population receiving both doses of the COVID-19 vaccine. The comparison of the timeline of infection and mortality rates show that despite the higher number of infections in the second and third waves, the mortality rates were much lower across Canada, compared to the first wave. The visualization of infections and morality against vaccinations show a declining in the infection and mortality rates as the percentage of fully vaccinated populace gradually increased.
